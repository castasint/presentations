Your agent doesn't have a retrieval problem. It has a **knowledge** problem.

Top-k vector search quietly caps what your agents can reason about.
Similar chunks aren't the same as connected facts.

Here's the distinction that changes the architecture:

A graph **database** is the engine — nodes, edges, first-class relationships, constant-time traversal.

A **knowledge graph** is the meaning — typed entities, an explicit ontology, facts as triples that support inference.

**Graph RAG** is the method — a retrieval technique that indexes a corpus into a graph and feeds the result to an LLM.

They stack. They are not interchangeable. Conflating them is how architectures go wrong.

Vectors find what's *similar*. Graphs find what's *connected*. Agents need both.

The carousel walks through 6 production-relevant patterns — hybrid retrieval, multi-hop traversal, graph memory, ontology-guided construction, community summarization, and KG-grounded verification — grounded in arXiv research with honest caveats on what the numbers actually show.
