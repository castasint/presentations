Quick Definitions

AI

Artificial Intelligence

The broadest field: any technique enabling machines to mimic human intelligence. Rule-based systems, search, planning, logic, learning.

Expert SystemsSearchPlanningLogic
DS

Data Science

Interdisciplinary practice of extracting knowledge from data. Stats, visualization, ML tools, domain expertise. Overlaps with ML but doesn't contain it.

StatisticsVisualizationEDASQL
ML

Machine Learning

A subset of AI where systems learn patterns from data. Includes both classical algorithms and deep neural networks.

SupervisedUnsupervisedSelf-Supervised
DL

Deep Learning

A subset of ML using neural networks with many layers. Excels at unstructured data: images, text, audio, video, graphs.

CNNsRNNsTransformersGNNs
LLM

LLMs

A subset of DL (NLP): massive transformer models trained on text. GPT-4, Claude, LLaMA. Great at language, not everything.

GPT-4ClaudeLLaMAGemini
Gen

Models that create new content: text, images, music, code. Includes LLMs, diffusion, GANs, VAEs, flow models. Not all GenAI is LLMs!

DiffusionGANsVAEsFlows

The Big Misconception

"Just Use an LLM"

Why this thinking costs you speed, money, and accuracy

❌ The Hype Trap

!Overkill: Using GPT-4 to classify 10k rows = burning money
!Slow: LLM inference is 1000x slower than XGBoost
!Unpredictable: LLMs hallucinate. Classical models don't.
!Opaque: Can't explain WHY an LLM made a decision
!Expensive: $10-30 per 1M tokens vs $0 for open-source ML
!Bad at: Math, precise logic, real-time predictions

✅ The Reality

Y80% of problems are solved by classical ML
YXGBoost + CSV beats LLM for structured data
YLinear regression still powers millions of forecasts
YRule engines are faster than LLMs for known logic
YCV models (YOLO, ResNet) beat LLMs at vision
YGNNs dominate drug discovery and fraud graphs
"The right tool for the right job."
LLMs are incredible at language understanding and generation. They are terrible at precise math, real-time prediction, computer vision, and small-data problems.

Emerging

Where Does Quantum ML Fit?

Q

Quantum Machine Learning

Algorithms that run on quantum computers or use quantum-inspired methods on classical hardware. Still largely experimental.

AQAOA: Quantum Approximate Optimization Algorithm
VVQE: Variational Quantum Eigensolver (chemistry)
KQuantum Kernels: Feature maps on quantum circuits
DQuantum Annealing: D-Wave for combinatorial optimization
HHamiltonian Simulation: Quantum systems modeling

Current Status: NISQ Era

~NISQ: Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum (50–1000 qubits)
!No proven quantum advantage for most ML tasks yet
YPromising: Quantum chemistry, optimization, simulation
~Timeline: 5–15 years to practical quantum ML at scale

The Quantum-Classical Hybrid

Most near-term QML uses variational circuits: quantum processors handle specific subroutines while classical optimizers tune parameters. Think of it as a quantum GPU accelerator — not a replacement for classical deep learning.

Bottom line: Quantum ML is fascinating research, but for production ML in 2025–2030, classical and deep learning methods remain dominant. Watch this space for optimization, materials science, and cryptography breakthroughs.

Supervised vs Unsupervised vs RL

SSupervised: Labeled data (input → known output). Classification, regression, forecasting.
UUnsupervised: No labels. Find hidden structure. Clustering, anomaly detection, PCA.
RReinforcement Learning: Learn by trial/error with rewards. Robotics, game AI, trading.
SSSelf-Supervised: Labels derived from data itself. Pre-training LLMs, contrastive learning.

Generative Model Types

TAutoregressive (LLMs): Predict next token. GPT, LLaMA, Claude.
DDiffusion: Iterative denoising. Stable Diffusion, DALL-E, Imagen.
GGANs: Generator vs Discriminator. Faces, style transfer. Hard to train.
VVAEs: Latent space learning. Generation + representation.
FFlow Models: Invertible transformations. High-quality density estimation.
History

Evolution of the Field

From rules to neural nets to LLMs — and what's next

1950s–60s
Symbolic AI
Rule-based systems, logic programming, expert systems. Turing Test proposed. No learning — pure human-encoded knowledge.
1980s–90s
Statistical ML
SVMs, Random Forests, Naive Bayes, k-means. The "learn from data" revolution. Data science emerges as a discipline.
1997
Deep Blue beats Kasparov
IBM's chess computer wins. Not ML — brute-force search + evaluation function. Shows power of classical AI + compute.
2012
Deep Learning Awakens
AlexNet wins ImageNet. GPUs + big data + ReLU make deep neural networks practical. CNNs dominate computer vision.
2014–15
GANs & Deep RL
GANs (Goodfellow) enable realistic image generation. DeepMind's DQN masters Atari games. AlphaGo (2016) beats Lee Sedol.
2017
Attention Is All You Need
Transformer architecture. Self-attention replaces RNNs. Parallel training at scale becomes possible. The LLM era begins.
2018–20
BERT & GPT Era
BERT (understanding) and GPT (generation) show power of pre-training. Transfer learning goes mainstream in NLP.
2020–21
Vision & Multimodal
Vision Transformers (ViT), CLIP (text+image), DALL-E (text-to-image). AI starts crossing modality boundaries.
2022–23
ChatGPT & GenAI Boom
GPT-3.5/4, Stable Diffusion, Midjourney. Generative AI goes consumer. The "LLM for everything" hype peaks.
2024–25
Agents & Efficiency
Agentic AI, RAG, small models (Phi, Llama-3.2), quantization, edge deployment. Cost and speed matter again.
2026+
Multimodal + Quantum?
True multimodal reasoning, reasoning models (o1-like), physical AI/robotics, early quantum-classical hybrid ML.
Pattern: Every decade, a new paradigm dominates. But old techniques don't die — they find their niche. Linear regression from the 1800s still powers more business decisions than GPT-4.
The Truth

What Actually Powers the World?

~80%
Classical ML & Stats

XGBoost, SQL, linear regression, random forest, heuristics. Running on CPUs in data centers everywhere.

~15%
Deep Learning

CV models, recommender systems, speech recognition, NLP (non-LLM). Running on GPUs.

~5%
LLMs / GenAI

Chatbots, content generation, coding assistants, RAG systems. Fastest-growing but smallest footprint.

🏆 The Unsung Heroes

1Linear Regression: Still the #1 forecasting tool in business
2SQL + Heuristics: Most "AI" dashboards are just smart queries
3Excel + Pivot Tables: More decisions made here than in Jupyter
4Rule Engines: Banks process billions of transactions with hardcoded rules
5A/B Testing: The real driver of product improvement at tech companies

🚀 Where LLMs Actually Shine

ADraft generation: First drafts of emails, docs, code, copy
BComplex reasoning: Multi-step analysis, planning, debugging
CKnowledge synthesis: Summarizing 100 pages in seconds
DNatural language interfaces: "Talk to your data"
ECreativity: Brainstorming, variant generation, storytelling
Cheat Sheet

Your Decision Memo

Start Simple

1Tabular data → XGBoost, Random Forest, Linear Models
2Need interpretability → Linear models, SHAP, decision trees
3Speed & cost critical → Classical ML on CPU
4Under 10k samples → Don't use deep learning
5Known business rules → Rule engine + heuristics

Go Deep

AImages / Video → CNNs, ViTs, YOLO, SAM
BText understanding → BERT, embeddings
CText generation → LLMs (but watch cost!)
DGames / Robotics → Deep RL (PPO, SAC)
EGraphs / Networks → GNNs (GraphSAGE, GAT)

Use LLMs When...

✓The task requires open-ended reasoning
✓You need to synthesize across diverse documents
✓Natural conversation is the interface
✓Budget allows $0.01–0.10+ per query

Avoid LLMs When...

✗You need deterministic, exact outputs
✗Latency must be <100ms
✗You have clean structured data and a clear target
✗You need fully explainable decisions (finance, healthcare)
"The best AI system is often a hybrid: classical ML for structured prediction, deep learning for perception, and LLMs for language — orchestrated together."
